Introducing WreckWatch©

Tech Instinct is in the development stage of a new product, WreckWatch. This new mobile phone application assists road users to alert emergency services and contacts in the unfortunate event of a road accident.

Overview

The number of vehicles has been increasing throughout the world and with this, sadly, so has the number of accidents (both fatal and non-fatal) involving vehicles. [1] This number of vehicle-related accidents can be safely said to be on the incline as per a report presented by the CDC. This reports key finding was that the number of traffic deaths has increased to 11.1 people per 100 000 people (from 2010-2019). There have also been cases where the family of the victim(s) and/or the emergency response team have not had the correct information in time, leading to preventable deaths/injuries and heart-broken family members. To decrease the average number of traffic related deaths throughout the world our project idea is to make use of smartphones. By allowing the smartphone to detect dangerous situations involving vehicles, such as traffic accidents, theft, fires etc, and sending correct relevant information to the appropriate authorities we hope to make the roads much safer than they are now.

Topic

Failing to provide timely lifesaving treatment to an injured person can be the leading cause of a road accident fatality. There is a clear need for a tool to help effectively call for assistance and to alert local emergency services while providing them with the precise location of the accident. This can be done by automating a request for help when the motion of a crash has been detected. The purpose of the following report is to document the production of a service that can address the need of a computerised technology that improves the response time of emergency services and their arrival to the scene of an accident. This product is a possible solution or improvement to the delayed arrival of emergency services, by providing the emergency dispatch with prompt, and precise details of the road accident.

Motivation

According to the Australian Institute of Health and Welfare, nearly two-thirds of all hospitalised injuries are accounted for by on road vehicle traffic crashes.1 “Road traffic injuries kill more than 1.25 million people every year and are the number one cause of death among 15–29 year olds. Over 50 million people are also injured in non-fatal crashes every year, causing an enormous burden of disability.” (World Health Organisation)2 Esurance reported that 77% of drivers have been involved in at least one road accident and out of the 10 million road accidents that occur every year, 3 of every thousand are fatalities.3 The process of requesting immediate assistance after being involved in a serious accident, has proven to be difficult. Currently, there are mobile applications that promise to assist the user to request for help from emergency services. However, there is a need for a simple, user-friendly smartphone application that can automate the processes involved with requesting for help and also provide quick and accurate details of the precise location an accident may have occurred. Using the in-built technology of a smartphone, it is possible to detect that a crash has happened and trigger these processes automatically. It is possible that those involved in an accident are unable to move or are unconscious and therefore, unable to call for help. By using a straightforward approach, this product development is a possible solution to this problem and can allow for the user to increase their chance of survival and decrease the severity of an injuries.

Aim

To implement an effective and efficient system for automated connectivity to emergency contacts and request the most immediate assistance in the event of a car accident.

The motivation behind this product idea is to minimize the casualty/injury rate of vehicle related accidents and to properly allow emergency responders to save people from said accidents more affectively. The way this idea works is by making use of android phones with a computerized framework which is used to identify vehicle-related accidents and alert the relevant authorities. The reason for why this framework will help minimize serious consequences of an accident is by decreasing the time between the accident occurring and the emergency responders finding out about it and gathering the relevant information. This is because the framework can immediately after being activated send the current location of the accident to the authorities withing the closest vicinity of the accident site, thus decreasing response time exponentially. By doing this emergency responders can help the victim(s) quicker and in turn, save more people.

In the event of a serious road accident, there are critical factors that can impact the severity of injuries that may have occurred. Taking every precaution to ensure that emergency services are contacted immediately could potentially save lives. The quicker emergency medical services are notified, the sooner they can arrive on the scene which means a greater the chance of survival. An effective post-crash response can significantly improve the outcome of someone who has been injured and needs immediate medical assistance.

Tech Instinct is in the development stage of a new product, WreckWatch. WreckWatch is a mobile phone application that assists road users to alert emergency services and contacts in the unfortunate event of a road accident. This automated service will use GPS to provide an accurate location and the in-built sensors/accelerometer in a smartphone to detect a crash. The intent of this product development is assist the user to promptly and accurately request for immediate assistance.

Plans and Progress

We will create a framework that utilizes Android smartphones and the OBD-II in vehicles. When this framework recognizes that the user has been involved in an accident it will alert client specific contacts via SMS. This SMS will contain relevant information, such as accident location, allowing for the appropriate personal people to be quickly notified and able to act accordingly. Along with the SMS, the framework will also make a call to the nearest emergency response authorities. However, for this framework to work the standard that Europe and the US has for their OBD-II needs to be met.

We could also create a framework that utilizes the accelerometer, GPS, and amplifier to detect accidents or connect a cable between the airbags system and telematics system to allow the Android phone to detect if the airbags have been deployed. However, with these methods the process after would be the same. After confirming that there has been an accident the framework would then notify a net server and send a SMS to the users preferred emergency contact(s), this also means that emergency response agencies need to have access to said webserver.

The current framework emergency response agencies have access to now utilizes the same sensors and equipment we would; however, one of the biggest drawbacks to their current framework is that they need to check the web server for accident notices, it is not automatic. In other words, there is no framework for personal responders which tracks a victim's location.

We would make use of a framework called WreckWatch which includes information from the accelerometer and acoustic information to from the receiver to identify an accident. The framework will, as previously stated, alert the relevant authorities, and can also send a voice message to the emergency responders closest to the accident site. However, this framework has a high chance of sending a false positive, which would be incredibly counterproductive for what we are trying to achieve. A false positive could be triggered by a framework error, dropping of the smartphone etc. To counteract this, we looked at pre-existing frameworks and will base our framework on these different ones to minimize the risk of a false positive emergency call as much as physically possible.

Smartphones are getting more technologically advanced every day, and the amount of smartphone users increases along with it. Because of this, we will centre this framework around smartphones to identify accidents and alert the relevant authorities. Smartphones also have the bonus of being most popular among teenagers and young adults, which coincidentally also happen to be the age groups most likely to be involved in a vehicle related accident. Therefore, by leveraging that smartphone are already integrated in the age group with the highest risk factor, we would minimize the hassle of having to allow for a large adjustment period among users of this framework.

As previously stated, smartphone technology has gotten astronomically better since their original release, meaning that it is far easier and more effective to leverage this technology to both identify accidents and automatically call for help. As an example, with even an older model smartphone, the iPhone 4; this phone incorporates a GPS framework which allows it to know the exact location of itself if it has a connection, an accelerometer, a microphone etc. However, now the technology in smartphones far surpasses that of the iPhone 4 with sensory information, meaning that it will be a lot more efficient for us to make use of this to identify an accident, alert authorities and further minimize the chance of getting a false positive. WIFI and the general reception area has gotten overall better and continues to get better, meaning that, even in remote areas, users can make use of this framework to get the help required in the event of an accident.

Scope and Limits

In Scope

  • Create an Emergency Contact database – the creation of a data structure that facilitates the input and organisation of emergency contact data according to specific criteria.
  • Create Emergency Message – this function takes current location data retrieved from GPS based location tracking information and inserts it into an emergency message.
  • Send Emergency Message to Emergency Contacts – a feature that simplifies the process of sending Emergency Message to multiple recipients at one time, using the contact information retrieved from the Emergency Contact database.

Out of Scope

  • Any deliverables, activities, core functionality, or rounds of revisions beyond what is outlined in the In Scope section above.
  • Training documentation – only basic demonstration will be provided.
  • Support and maintenance of the implemented system once the project has been finalised.
  • The data entry – adding the actual data to the Emergency Contact database.
  • Creation of a developer account, or any registration to application store/s services.
  • Creation of link or any association to services that provide public with access to downloading a copy of the finalised project.

Tools and Technologies

The key technology of this project is Arduino. This IOT gadget acts as a bridge to operate and connect every key component together. Familiarity with developing Arduino and electrical safety are strictly required.

Environment data including the vehicle’s motion, speed and location, these data can be collected by using accelerometer and GPS module.

GY-251 module has a built-in MPU-6050 which is use for measuring velocity, orientation and gravitational forces.

GPS module: NEO-6M

Physical and Hardware Components:

Arduino Mini: a compact version of Arduino, a microcontroller that is able to have computation power and interact with other electronic devices. In this project, this IOT gadget is the key component which acts as a bridge to operate and connect every key component together. Familiarity with developing Arduino and electrical safety are strictly required.



Accelerometer and Gyroscope Sensor: GY-251. GY-251 module has a built-in MPU-6050 which is use for measuring velocity, orientation and gravitational forces. This component is used for detecting sudden changes in the vehicle’s movement to identify likely car crashes.



GPS module: NEO-6M. A module built for performing GPS (global positioning system) activities. This component capable of communicating with GPS satellites and locating. This component is to collect the positions of vehicles that happen to be in an accident.

This project is built and based on the current smartphones running on Android and IOS operating systems, which are the most common OS in the smartphone industry.

Cloud Services:

Amazon Web Services (AWS) provides a wide range of cloud services which can be utilised for many business models. AWS services are highly scalable and available across nations worldwide. In this project, the following services will be used:

  • Amazon Aurora: a distributed relational database service with high availability. With storage auto-scaling and automatic replication, Aurora gains high fault-tolerance. Aurora is compatible with MySQL, which will be used as the main database for this project.
  • Elastic Beanstalk: a deployment environment for web applications. This service is a combination of other AWS services which assists the users on configuration and scaling. Elastic Beanstalk is serverless since users only need to deploy and the service will handle most of configuration and allocation for healthy web applications. This service is utilised for maintaining the website for the project.
  • Lambda: a serverless computing service. It acts as functions that execute the code and automatically manage resources for computing. This service is used for executing algorithms, managing requests between components and the web, and sending requests for email and SMS notifications.
  • Simple Notification Service (SNS), Simple Email Service (SES): web services for sending emails and SMS messages. These two services are used as a notification system of the project.

Testing

At Wreckwatch our goals are to make a technology that helps provide emergency services and people a more direct way to inform the required services needed to help in a very distressing situation, this cannot be done without testing our product to ensure this can be delivered. One question that needs to be answered or tested by WreckWatch is which part of the globe this product will be most effective in. By using statistics on car accidents and where most injuries and deaths occur in the world would provide a good indicator of where WreckWatch would be most suitable to implement the product and where it would have the greatest impact to help emergency services and in effect help save lives that may be in danger from these accidents. Social media would be used to take in surveys from users to see if this is a product that the public would use and this could be also be useful to find out the best areas of people would be most interested in using the product. The most crucial part of this product working is to figure out if using an OBDII sensor with an application or IoS device in a crash scenario will be effective at relaying the response via SMS to emergency services. This can be done by setting up a standard crash test scenario with dummies and the use of the WreckWatch system installed into the vehicle to make sure that everything works accordingly without any bugs as to not have false positives and being counterproductive in what WreckWatch is trying to achieve.
